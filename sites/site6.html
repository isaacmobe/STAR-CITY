Elephant Kingdom

Founded: Ancient times

Population: Approximately 500 elephants

History: Elephant Kingdom is a sanctuary that has been home to elephants since ancient times. Located in a lush forest, this kingdom provides a safe haven for these majestic creatures. Historically, it served as a ceremonial site for rituals and gatherings, showcasing the deep cultural significance of elephants in the region.

Special Attractions:

  • The Elephant Trails: Guided paths through the forest where visitors can observe elephants in their natural habitat.
  • The Elephant Feeding Sessions: Interactive sessions where visitors can feed elephants under supervision.
  • The Elephant Conservation Center: Educational facility focused on conservation efforts and research.
  • The Elephant Watchtower: Observation tower offering panoramic views of the sanctuary.

Site Coordinator: Dr. William Parker

Dr. William Parker is the current site coordinator for Elephant Kingdom. With a PhD in wildlife conservation and a passion for elephants, Dr. Parker oversees conservation projects and educational programs at the sanctuary. His expertise ensures sustainable practices and enhances visitor experiences through informative tours and workshops.
