Mobe Fossil Site

Discovered: 1987

Location: Mobe Valley

History: The Mobe Fossil Site was discovered in 1987 in Mobe Valley. It is renowned for its rich deposits of fossils dating back millions of years, providing valuable insights into ancient life forms and environmental conditions. The site has been instrumental in paleontological research and continues to attract scientists and enthusiasts interested in Earth's prehistoric past.

Special Attractions:

  • The Fossil Exhibits: Displays of fossilized remains including dinosaurs, ancient plants, and marine creatures.
  • The Digging Experience: Guided tours offering visitors a chance to participate in fossil excavation under expert supervision.
  • The Paleontology Workshops: Educational programs where participants learn about fossil preservation and analysis techniques.
  • The Geological Trail: Interpretive trail showcasing geological formations and fossil discovery sites.

Site Curator: Dr. Michael Roberts

Dr. Michael Roberts is the curator of the Mobe Fossil Site. With a PhD in paleontology and extensive field experience, Dr. Roberts oversees research activities and public outreach programs at the site. His expertise ensures the preservation of fossils and the dissemination of knowledge about Earth's ancient history.
